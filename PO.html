Program Outcomes (POs), Program Educational Objectives (PEOs) & Program Specific Outcomes (PSOs)

Program Outcomes (POs) – Cyber Security

PO1 – Engineering Knowledge
Apply the knowledge of mathematics, science, engineering fundamentals, and cybersecurity principles to solve complex information security problems.

PO2 – Problem Analysis
Identify, analyze, and interpret security threats and vulnerabilities using domain-specific tools to provide effective solutions.

PO3 – Design & Development of Solutions
Design and implement secure systems, processes, and applications that meet specified requirements while considering public health, safety, cultural, societal, and environmental factors.

PO4 – Conduct Investigations of Complex Problems
Use research-based knowledge and methods, including data collection, analysis, and experiments, to draw valid conclusions in cybersecurity.

PO5 – Modern Tool Usage
Select, adapt, and apply appropriate modern tools, open-source platforms, and emerging technologies for secure data management and cyber defense.

PO6 – The Engineer and Society
Apply contextual knowledge to assess societal, legal, and cultural issues and responsibilities related to cybersecurity and ethical hacking practices.

PO7 – Environment and Sustainability
Understand the impact of cybersecurity solutions on sustainable digital development and demonstrate the need for green and energy-efficient technologies.

PO8 – Ethics
Commit to professional ethics, responsibilities, and cyber laws, adhering to global standards and values in information security practices.

PO9 – Individual and Team Work
Function effectively as an individual, and as a member or leader in diverse teams and multidisciplinary environments for secure digital ecosystem development.

PO10 – Communication Skills
Communicate effectively on complex cybersecurity activities with the technical community and society, including effective documentation, reports, design documentation, and presentations.

PO11 – Project Management and Finance
Demonstrate knowledge of management principles and apply them to one’s own work as a member or leader in a team to manage cybersecurity projects in multidisciplinary environments.

PO12 – Life-long Learning
Recognize the need for, and have the preparation and ability to engage in independent and life-long learning in the broadest context of technological change in cybersecurity and information security.

Program Educational Objectives (PEOs) – Cyber Security

PEO1 – Professional Excellence
Graduates will acquire strong knowledge and practical skills in cybersecurity to solve complex real-world problems at national and global levels.

PEO2 – Ethical and Social Responsibility
Graduates will uphold ethical values, cyber laws, and societal responsibilities while ensuring secure digital practices and data protection.

PEO3 – Innovation and Lifelong Learning
Graduates will engage in continuous learning, research, and innovation to develop automated and advanced solutions in digital security systems.

Program Specific Outcomes (PSOs) – Cyber Security

PSO1 – Secure System Development
Graduates will design, implement, and manage secure computer networks, applications, and digital infrastructures against cyber threats.

PSO2 – Threat Analysis and Forensics
Graduates will analyze, investigate, and mitigate cybersecurity incidents using modern tools, forensic methods, and risk assessment strategies.

PSO3 – Ethical Hacking and Compliance
Graduates will apply ethical hacking techniques, penetration testing, and comply with cybersecurity laws, standards, and international frameworks.

Program Outcomes (POs) – Information Security

PO1 – Engineering & Security Knowledge
Apply knowledge of mathematics, science, and information security principles to solve complex security problems.

PO2 – Problem Analysis
Identify, analyze, and interpret information security risks, vulnerabilities, and threats using appropriate methodologies.

PO3 – Design & Development of Solutions
Design secure frameworks, protocols, and systems to meet organizational and societal information security needs.

PO4 – Conduct Investigations of Complex Problems
Use research methods, data analysis, and security testing to investigate and provide valid conclusions for information security issues.

PO5 – Modern Tool Usage
Select, adapt, and apply appropriate modern IT tools, open-source platforms, and emerging technologies for secure data management.

PO6 – The Engineer and Society
Apply contextual knowledge to assess societal, legal, and cultural issues and responsibilities in ensuring safe information practices.

PO7 – Environment and Sustainability
Understand the impact of information security practices on sustainability and promote eco-friendly digital technologies.

PO8 – Ethics
Demonstrate professional ethics, compliance with cyber laws, and commitment to integrity in securing organizational and personal data.

PO9 – Individual and Team Work
Function effectively as an individual, team member, or leader in multidisciplinary environments for developing secure information systems.

PO10 – Communication Skills
Communicate effectively with professionals and society on complex information security topics through clear documentation and presentations.

PO11 – Project Management and Finance
Apply management principles and financial understanding to lead projects and deliver cost-effective information security solutions.

PO12 – Lifelong Learning
Recognize the importance of continuous learning and adapt to evolving tools, standards, and technologies in information security.

Program Educational Objectives (PEOs) – Information Security

PEO1 – Professional Competence
Graduates will acquire strong foundational knowledge and practical expertise in information security to address global challenges.

PEO2 – Ethical and Social Responsibility
Graduates will uphold ethical values, legal compliance, and societal responsibilities while ensuring secure management of information.

PEO3 – Innovation and Lifelong Learning
Graduates will engage in lifelong learning, research, and innovation to design automated and sustainable solutions in information security systems.

Program Specific Outcomes (PSOs) – Information Security

PSO1 – Secure Framework Development
Graduates will design, develop, and implement secure frameworks, policies, and digital infrastructures for protecting organizational data.

PSO2 – Threat Analysis and Incident Response
Graduates will analyze, investigate, and mitigate information security incidents using forensic methods, risk assessment, and modern security tools.

PSO3 – Compliance and Ethical Hacking
Graduates will apply ethical hacking, penetration testing, and comply with laws, standards, and best practices in information security.
